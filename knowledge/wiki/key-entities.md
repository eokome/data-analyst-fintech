## Most-Complained-About Companies

### Credit Reporting Agencies (Highest Volume Overall)
The three major bureaus are the most complained-about entities in the entire CFPB database, accounting for the majority of the 80.3% of FY2023 complaints tied to credit reporting:
- **Equifax** — errors, investigation failures, data breach aftermath
- **Experian** — incorrect reporting, dispute resolution failures
- **TransUnion** — inaccurate data, difficulty correcting errors

### Credit Card Issuers (High-Growth Complaint Category)
Complaint volume surged 67% in 2024. Top complainees by market share:
- **JPMorgan Chase** — largest US card issuer; highest raw complaint volume
- **Citibank** — high complaint volume; rewards and billing disputes prominent
- **Capital One** — significant complaint history; rewards and fee issues
- **Bank of America** — consistent top-10 complainee
- **Synchrony Financial** — private label cards (31.3% avg APR); outsized complaint rate per account
- **Discover** — rewards and billing dispute complaints

### Mortgage Servicers (Rate-Sensitive Category)
Servicing complaints dominate; top complainees by average monthly volume:
- **Wells Fargo** — consistently highest mortgage complaint volume
- **Bank of America** — second highest
- **Ocwen / PHH Mortgage** — subject to $2 billion CFPB relief order; parent Onity Group leads reverse mortgage complaints (95 in 2024)
- **Finance of America** — 27 reverse mortgage complaints in 2024
- **Longbridge Financial** — 11 reverse mortgage complaints in 2024

### Debt Collectors
Second largest complaint category overall; key players include large third-party debt collection agencies and debt buyers. Common complaint trigger: attempting to collect debts not owed.

## Geographic Patterns

The CFPB database includes state-level data enabling geographic targeting. Key patterns from the data:

### Highest-Volume States (Raw Count)
States with the largest populations generate the most complaints:
- **California** — largest US state; highest raw complaint volume
- **Florida** — large population, high mortgage activity, large elderly population (reverse mortgage complaints)
- **Texas** — fast-growing; high mortgage origination volume
- **New York** — dense financial services market
- **Georgia** — Atlanta is a major financial services hub; historically high debt collection complaints

### High Rate Per Capita
Some states generate complaints at rates disproportionate to population:
- **Southern states** (Mississippi, Alabama, Louisiana) — higher payday lending rates relative to population
- **States with high ARM penetration** — elevated mortgage complaints during rate hike cycles
- **States with large military populations** (Virginia, North Carolina) — servicemember-specific financial product complaints

### Geographic Insight for Lead Gen
States with high complaint rates per capita but underserved lead generation coverage represent the highest-opportunity markets for EPCVIP. Complaint density is a proxy for consumer pain and search intent.

## Regulatory Entities

### CFPB (Primary)
- Jurisdiction: banks >$10B assets, all non-bank financial entities (payday lenders, debt collectors, mortgage servicers, credit reporting agencies)
- Tools: supervision, enforcement, rulemaking, public complaint database
- Notable actions: $2B Ocwen order, $191M Tempoe order, $21B+ total consumer relief

### Federal Reserve
- Jurisdiction: state member banks >$10B assets (non-CFPB consumer laws); all state member banks ≤$10B (all consumer laws)
- Role: TILA, ECOA, Fair Housing Act enforcement; coordinates with CFPB on systemic issues
- Complaint handling: separate from CFPB for its supervised entities

### FTC
- Jurisdiction: non-bank financial companies; broad fraud mandate
- Scale: 3 million fraud reports received in 2025; $15.9B in consumer losses; $1.8B in FY2025 redress
- Overlap with CFPB: debt collection, credit reporting, advertising truth

### State Regulators
- Handle complaints for smaller institutions not covered by CFPB/Fed
- Often have parallel complaint systems (especially NY DFS, CA DFPI)

## EPCVIP as a Key Entity

EPCVIP sits at the intersection of all entities above. As a financial lead generation company:

**Company profile:**
- **CEO/Founder:** Valeriy Zeltser
- **HQ:** Calabasas, CA
- **Employees:** ~75
- **Revenue:** ~$5.6 million
- **Recognition:** Named Top Featured Vendor in Financial Services Review (2022)

**What it does:**
- Connects consumers actively seeking financial products with advertiser-lenders
- Generates tens of thousands of leads daily across credit cards, loans, mortgages
- Operates a proprietary optimization platform (highest EPCs in industry)

**Why CFPB data matters to EPCVIP:**
- Complaint spikes identify products where consumers are switching → hot lead gen opportunities
- Geographic complaint density maps to high-intent geographic markets
- Company-level complaint data identifies which lenders are losing consumer trust → opportunity for competitors in EPCVIP's advertiser network
- Rate hike periods = mortgage refinance lead spikes = peak campaign value windows

**EPCVIP's compliance exposure:**
- As a lead gen operator, subject to FTC truth-in-advertising standards
- Must ensure landing pages don't make deceptive claims about rates or approval likelihood
- Data collection and transfer practices governed by FTC rules (CAN-SPAM, TCPA)
