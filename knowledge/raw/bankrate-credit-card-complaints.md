# Credit Card Complaints — Bankrate / CFPB Analysis

**Source URL:** https://www.bankrate.com/finance/credit-cards/credit-card-complaints/ (page returned 404; content sourced from CFPB reports and web search)  
**Researched:** 2026-04-27

## Overview

Credit card complaints to the CFPB have surged dramatically in recent years, driven by rewards disputes, high interest rates, and billing practices. The 2024 data shows a 67% increase in complaints about general purpose credit cards or charge cards.

## Key 2024 Statistics

- Complaints about general purpose credit cards increased **67% in 2024**
- CFPB sent complaints to more than **3,600 companies** for review and response in 2024
- CFPB's consumer complaint database gathered nearly **5 million submissions** from January 2020 through September 2024
- Average APR: **25.2%** for general purpose cards (highest since at least 2015)
- Average APR: **31.3%** for private label credit cards
- Consumers assessed **$160 billion in interest charges** in 2024 (up from $105 billion in 2022)

## Top Complaint Categories

### Credit Card Rewards Issues
- Dramatic increase in rewards-related complaints in recent years
- CFPB launched "bait-and-switch" investigation targeting card issuers over rewards programs
- Nearly **1 in 4 cardholders (23%)** did not redeem any rewards at all in 2024 (Bankrate survey)

### Billing and Interest Charges
- Surprise interest charges despite timely payment
- Junk fees and unauthorized charges
- APR increases without adequate notice

### Credit Reporting
- Errors appearing on credit reports tied to credit card accounts
- Difficulty disputing incorrect information

## Top Complained-About Companies

Based on CFPB complaint volume, the largest issuers receive the most complaints (scaled to market share):
- JPMorgan Chase
- Citibank
- Capital One
- Bank of America
- Synchrony Financial (private label cards)
- Discover

## Regulatory Context

The CFPB has used complaint data to:
- Investigate bait-and-switch credit card rewards practices (December 2024)
- Push for rules capping late fees
- Publish the 2025 Consumer Credit Card Market Report (Federal Register, January 2026)

## Lead Gen Implications

High APR environments and rewards disputes signal consumer dissatisfaction — consumers actively shopping for better credit card offers. CFPB complaint spikes in credit cards correlate with elevated consumer intent to switch products, a prime lead generation opportunity.
