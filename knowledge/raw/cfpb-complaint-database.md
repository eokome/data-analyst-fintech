# CFPB Consumer Complaint Database

**Source URL:** https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/consumer-complaints/  
**Scraped:** 2026-04-27

## What is the Database?

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) operates a public database of consumer complaints about financial products and services. It enables anyone to explore how companies respond to consumer issues across the financial sector.

## Key Statistics

- **Response Rate:** 98% of complaints sent to companies get timely responses
- **Publication Timeline:** Complaints are published after companies respond or 15 days pass, whichever comes first
- **Update Frequency:** The database generally updates daily
- **Total Consumer Relief:** $19.7 billion in relief ordered (as of January 30, 2025)
- **2022 Volume:** CFPB sent more than 820,000 of nearly 1.3 million complaints to 3,200 companies
- **2024 Volume:** Sent complaints to more than 3,600 companies for review and response

## How It Works

### Complaint Submission & Processing

Consumers submit complaints through the CFPB. The bureau then sends eligible complaints to companies for response. Only complaints that receive company responses (or timeout after 15 days) become public.

### What Gets Published

The database publishes:
- Complaint details and company responses
- Consumer narratives (with personal information removed)
- Product/service categories and issues
- Response timelines and company actions

**Excluded:** Complaints about depository institutions with less than $10 billion in assets are referred to other regulators.

## Top Complaint Categories (FY2023)

The top three issues all related to credit reporting:
1. Incorrect information on credit reports
2. Improper use of credit reports
3. Problems with a credit reporting company's investigation into existing problems

These three issues made up **80.3% of all complaints** received during FY2023. The single most common issue was incorrect information on credit reports (38% of all complaints).

## Data Access

**Free Download Options:**
- CSV or JSON format of all complaints
- Filtered subsets by product, issue, or company
- API access for programmatic use

**Exploration Tools:**
- Trends visualization by time period
- Geographic mapping
- Searchable complaint narratives
- Data filtering and sorting

## Important Caveats

The CFPB emphasizes this data represents complaints filed, not statistical proof of widespread issues. Factors affecting volume include company size, market share, and population density. The bureau recommends combining complaint data with other datasets for context.

## Data Applications

The CFPB uses complaint insights to:
- Identify marketplace problems
- Enforce consumer financial laws
- Develop regulatory guidance
- Educate consumers

## Annual Reporting

Each spring, the CFPB reports complaint trends to Congress covering the previous year's data.
