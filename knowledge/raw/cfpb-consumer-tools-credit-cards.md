# CFPB Consumer Tools — Credit Cards

**Source URL:** https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/credit-cards/  
**Scraped:** 2026-04-27

## Overview

The CFPB provides comprehensive credit card management resources for consumers. The agency states they "make sure banks, lenders, and other financial companies treat you fairly."

## Getting Started

### Educational Basics

The CFPB covers foundational credit card knowledge:

- **Terms Changes:** Companies typically can modify future card terms with "45 days in advance notice of any significant changes"
- **Interest Calculations:** Most issuers calculate rates daily; paying balances faster reduces interest charges
- **APR Types:** Consumers should distinguish between fixed rates and variable rates tied to interest indexes

## Understanding Your Situation

### Common Issues Addressed
- Low credit limits
- Interest charges despite timely full payment
- Declined transactions and authorization holds

### Consumer Rights
- Existing balance interest rates cannot increase unless payment is overdue
- Lost/stolen card users aren't liable for unauthorized charges when reported promptly

### Dispute & Resolution Resources

The CFPB accepts complaints and typically "work to get you a response, generally within 15 days."

## Market Context (2024)

- Average APR reached 25.2% for general purpose cards and 31.3% for private label credit cards (highest since at least 2015)
- Consumers were assessed $160 billion in interest charges in 2024, up from $105 billion in 2022
- Complaints about general purpose credit cards increased 67% in 2024
- Nearly 1 in 4 cardholders (23%) did not redeem any rewards at all in 2024
