## What Is the CFPB?

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) is a U.S. government agency dedicated to ensuring fair treatment by financial institutions. The bureau aims to "make consumer financial markets work for consumers, responsible providers, and the economy as a whole."

The agency protects consumers by combating "unfair, deceptive, or abusive practices" and enforces action against law-breaking companies. It provides consumers with "information, steps, and tools" necessary for informed financial decisions.

## History

- Concept originally proposed in 2007 by Elizabeth Warren
- Created by the Dodd–Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (2010)
- Response to the 2008 financial crisis
- Officially opened July 21, 2011
- Consolidated responsibilities from seven separate federal agencies

## What the CFPB Regulates

The CFPB has jurisdiction over financial products and services including:
- Mortgages (origination and servicing)
- Credit cards
- Student loans
- Auto loans
- Payday loans and small-dollar lending
- Debt collection
- Credit reporting
- Prepaid cards and remittances
- Checking accounts and savings accounts

## Core Functions

1. **Rulemaking:** Implements federal consumer financial law; writes rules to prevent unfair and deceptive practices
2. **Supervision:** Examines financial companies for compliance
3. **Enforcement:** Takes legal action against violators
4. **Consumer complaints:** Accepts and forwards consumer complaints; facilitates resolution
5. **Financial education:** "Ask CFPB" database with 1,000+ questions answered (63 million+ users)
6. **Research:** Publishes market research and data reports

## How It Helps Consumers

**Complaint Handling:**
- Consumers can submit complaints to CFPB when direct company contact fails
- 98% of forwarded complaints receive timely responses
- CFPB works on consumers' behalf to contact institutions

**Financial Relief:**
- $21 billion+ returned to consumers through enforcement
- 205 million+ consumers/accounts benefited
- Medical debt collection removed from 22.8 million people's credit reports
- $6.1 billion annual savings from overdraft and NSF fee reductions

**Consumer Rights Information:**
- Plain-language explanations of rights under TILA, ECOA, FCRA, FDCPA
- Available in 8 languages
- Free tools for comparing mortgages, credit cards, student loans

## Current Status (2025–2026)

The CFPB faces significant political headwinds under the Trump administration:
- Significant reduction in enforcement activity
- Some staff reductions and operational changes
- Court cases ongoing regarding the agency's structure and funding
- Deprioritized certain enforcement areas (e.g., Buy Now Pay Later)
- Despite disruptions, complaint database remains operational and public

## Why It Matters for Analytics

The CFPB is the primary source of structured, public data on consumer financial dissatisfaction in the United States. Its complaint database (updated daily) offers:
- Product-level complaint counts
- Company-level complaint data
- Geographic breakdown by state
- Issue taxonomy for drill-down analysis
- Consumer narratives for qualitative insight
