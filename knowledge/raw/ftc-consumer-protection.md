# FTC — Consumer Protection

**Source URL:** https://www.ftc.gov/consumer-protection (page returned 403; content sourced from FTC web search)  
**Researched:** 2026-04-27

## Mission

The FTC's consumer protection mission is to fight fraud, deception, and unfair business practices in the marketplace. As the nation's consumer protection agency, the FTC has a broad mandate fulfilled through:
- Aggressive law enforcement actions
- Consumer and business education
- Policy reports and guidance
- Workshops and rulemaking

## Scale of Consumer Harm (2025 Data)

- **3 million fraud reports** received by the FTC in 2025
- **$15.9 billion** in consumer losses reported in 2025 (up from $12 billion prior year)
- Largest single category: **Investment scams** ($7.9 billion in losses)
- **40 law enforcement actions** brought in FY2025 covering fraudulent schemes
- **$1.8 billion** in consumer redress obtained in FY2025

## Key Enforcement Areas

### FY2025 Actions Targeted
- Business opportunity and investment fraud
- Unlawful robocalls
- Technical support scams
- Government or business impersonation fraud
- Unfair or deceptive fees

### Impersonation Rule Enforcement
- Government and Business Impersonation Rule took effect in 2024
- In first year: five cases filed, 13 websites illegally impersonating the FTC shut down

## Relationship to CFPB

The FTC and CFPB serve overlapping but distinct consumer protection roles:
- **FTC:** Broad consumer protection across all sectors; primary jurisdiction over non-bank financial companies not covered by CFPB
- **CFPB:** Specific to consumer financial products and services; complements FTC for financial sector
- Both agencies coordinate enforcement, particularly on debt collection, credit reporting, and fintech

## Relevant to Financial Lead Generation

The FTC's enforcement of advertising truth standards (Truth in Advertising) directly affects the lead generation industry. EPCVIP and similar companies must ensure:
- Lead quality claims are accurate
- Landing pages don't make deceptive representations
- Data collection practices comply with FTC rules (CAN-SPAM, TCPA coordination)
- No misleading claims about rates, terms, or approval likelihood

## Consumer Fraud Report Data

The FTC Consumer Sentinel Network publishes annual data on fraud by category and geography — complementary to CFPB complaint data for identifying high-risk markets and consumer pain points.
