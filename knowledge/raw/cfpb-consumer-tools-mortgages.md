# CFPB Consumer Tools — Mortgages

**Source URL:** https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/mortgages/  
**Scraped:** 2026-04-27

## Overview

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) provides comprehensive mortgage guidance for homebuyers, current mortgage holders, and those facing payment difficulties.

## Key Resource Categories

### Getting a Mortgage

- **Buying a House Tools:** The CFPB offers guidance "Whether you're just thinking about buying a home or about to close" to help consumers "take control of the process" through their Owning a Home initiative.

### Maintaining Your Mortgage

#### Understanding Your Mortgage
- **Monthly Statement Management:** Resources explain how to "pay your mortgage on time, every time" and understand what appears on statements.
- **Payment Changes:** Guidance covers reasons payments increase, including "adjustable rate mortgage and the interest rate changed."

#### Key Terminology
The CFPB defines essential concepts including:
- Automatic payment
- Escrow accounts
- Force-placed insurance
- Forbearance and loan modification
- Foreclosure protections
- Refinancing options
- Reverse mortgages
- Servicer responsibilities

### Trouble Paying Your Mortgage

The bureau recommends: "reach out to your mortgage servicer and a HUD-approved housing counselor" when facing payment difficulties.

**Options include:**
- Four-step foreclosure avoidance guidance
- Mortgage servicer loss mitigation obligations
- Escrow account problem resolution
- Servicer company change procedures

## Consumer Protections

### Rights & Protections
- Servicers must assist borrowers in "find[ing] ways to avoid foreclosure"
- "Foreclosure protections under state and federal law" apply to borrowers
- Inherited home provisions require servicers to explain ownership documentation

### Recent CFPB Actions
- Updated mortgage servicing rules for enhanced borrower protections
- $2 billion relief order against Ocwen for servicing violations
- Enforcement against deceptive mortgage advertising

## Mortgage Complaint Data

- Mortgages accounted for 3,762 complaints in a recent monthly snapshot
- About 43% of complaints related to "trouble during the payment process"
- Top servicers with the most complaints: Wells Fargo, Bank of America, Ocwen
- Key issues: servicers pressuring borrowers to take higher-interest loans, delays in processing requests, denial of requests to remove original borrowers
