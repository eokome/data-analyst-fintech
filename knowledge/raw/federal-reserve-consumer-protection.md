# Federal Reserve — Consumer Protection Programs

**Source URL:** https://www.federalreserve.gov/consumerscommunities/consumer_protection.htm (page returned 404; content sourced from Federal Reserve web search)  
**Researched:** 2026-04-27

## Overview

The Federal Reserve promotes consumer protection, financial inclusion, and community development through targeted work in supervision, regulatory policy, and research and analysis. It operates in a complementary role to the CFPB, retaining supervisory authority over state member banks.

## Supervisory Responsibilities

The Federal Reserve retains supervisory responsibility for:
1. **State member banks with consolidated assets over $10 billion** — compliance with consumer protection laws not specifically transferred to the CFPB
2. **State member banks with consolidated assets of $10 billion or less** — compliance with ALL consumer protection laws
3. **Bank holding companies** — serves as consolidated supervisor; consumer compliance risk incorporated into holding company ratings

## Key Regulations Enforced

Consumer protection supervision program covers:
- Truth in Lending Act (TILA)
- Electronic Fund Transfer Act
- Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)
- Fair Housing Act (FHA)
- Prohibition on unfair or deceptive acts or practices (UDAP) under the FTC Act

## Examination and Complaint Processes

In addition to on-site examiner reviews, Federal Reserve staff:
- Identify and investigate possible violations of consumer protection laws
- Answer consumers' questions and explain consumer rights under federal law
- Investigate complaints against entities supervised by the Federal Reserve
- Refer complaints about other entities to the appropriate agency

## Relationship to CFPB

The Federal Reserve and CFPB have complementary but distinct jurisdictions:
- **CFPB** handles larger institutions (>$10 billion assets) and non-bank entities
- **Federal Reserve** retains oversight of smaller state member banks
- Both agencies coordinate on fair lending, ECOA, and systemic consumer protection issues
- The Fed's Consumer Affairs program handles the complaint intake for its supervised entities

## Key Research Output

The Federal Reserve publishes:
- Annual Consumer and Community Affairs reports
- Consumer Compliance Handbook (supervisory manual)
- "Fed Explained" consumer education series
- Research on credit access, financial inclusion, and community development

## Relevance to Lead Gen Analytics

The Fed's data on credit markets, interest rates, and consumer financial conditions provides macroeconomic context for CFPB complaint trends. Rate hike decisions (e.g., 2022–2023 cycle) directly correlate with mortgage and credit card complaint surges tracked in the CFPB database.
