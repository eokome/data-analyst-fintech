## What the CFPB Complaint Database Is

The CFPB Consumer Complaint Database is a public, searchable repository of complaints consumers have filed against financial companies. NerdWallet and other personal finance outlets regularly analyze it to identify patterns, top offenders, and consumer trends.

## How It Works (Consumer Perspective)

1. Consumer submits complaint via CFPB website or phone
2. CFPB reviews and forwards complaint to the financial company
3. Company has 15 days to respond
4. Complaint and response become public in the database
5. Database updates daily

## Key Data Points

- **$19.7 billion** in consumer relief ordered (as of January 2025)
- **98%** of complaints forwarded to companies receive timely responses
- **FY2023:** Top three complaint types all related to credit reporting, comprising **80.3%** of all complaints
  - Incorrect information on credit reports
  - Improper use of credit reports
  - Problems with investigation into existing issues
- **Most common single issue:** Incorrect information on credit reports (38% of all complaints)
- Database is searchable by company, product, issue, state, and date

## What Consumers Are Complaining About Most (Recent Trends)

Per NerdWallet analysis:
- **Credit reporting** dominates — driven by errors and disputes with Equifax, Experian, TransUnion
- **Debt collection** — second largest category
- **Credit cards** — surging 67% in 2024, driven by rewards disputes and high APRs
- **Mortgages** — servicing complaints spike during rate hike periods
- **Personal loans / payday loans** — persistent complaint source

## How to Use the Database

**For consumers:**
- Search by company to see complaint history before choosing a financial product
- Submit complaints to escalate disputes that direct company contact hasn't resolved
- Access as CSV/JSON for data analysis

**For businesses (including lead gen):**
- Identify which companies and products have highest complaint rates
- Track complaint trends to spot consumer pain points
- Use geographic data to identify high-dissatisfaction markets

## Limitations

- Not a statistical sample — volume correlates with company size and market share
- Complaints are unverified; companies can dispute narratives
- Excludes institutions under $10 billion in assets (referred to other regulators)
- Published data has ~15-day lag
