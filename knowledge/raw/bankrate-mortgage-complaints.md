# Mortgage Complaints — Bankrate / CFPB Analysis

**Source URL:** https://www.bankrate.com/mortgages/mortgage-complaints/ (page returned 404; content sourced from CFPB reports and web search)  
**Researched:** 2026-04-27

## Overview

Mortgage complaints to the CFPB reflect systemic issues in loan servicing, particularly during periods of payment stress. The complaint landscape is dominated by servicing problems rather than origination disputes.

## Key Statistics

- Mortgages accounted for **3,762 complaints** in the CFPB's most recent monthly snapshot
- About **43% of complaints** relate to "trouble during the payment process"
- Reverse mortgage complaints: **298 total in 2024** (down 63 from 2023)

## Top Complained-About Servicers

Companies receiving the most average monthly complaints:
1. **Wells Fargo** — consistently highest complaint volume
2. **Bank of America** — second highest
3. **Ocwen** — significant complaint history; CFPB ordered $2 billion in consumer relief
4. **Onity Group Inc.** (PHH Mortgage / Liberty Reverse Mortgage) — 95 reverse mortgage complaints in 2024
5. **Finance of America** — 27 reverse mortgage complaints
6. **Longbridge Financial** — 11 reverse mortgage complaints

## Top Issues

1. **Trouble during payment process** (43% of complaints)
   - Difficulty contacting servicers to fix issues
   - Payment application errors
   - Escrow account mismanagement

2. **Servicer misconduct**
   - Pressure from servicers to take out higher-interest loans
   - Repeated delays in processing modification requests
   - Denial of requests to remove original borrowers from mortgages
   - Sending account information to abusers in domestic violence cases

3. **Foreclosure-related**
   - Loss mitigation failures
   - Improper foreclosure initiation
   - Inadequate disclosure of options

## Rate Environment Impact

Mortgage complaints track closely with rate hikes. When the Federal Reserve raised rates aggressively in 2022–2023, adjustable-rate mortgage borrowers saw payment increases, driving complaint volume higher. The CFPB specifically flags "adjustable rate mortgage and the interest rate changed" as a key trigger for consumer distress.

## Regulatory Actions

- **Ocwen**: $2 billion relief order for servicing violations
- **CFPB mortgage servicing rule updates**: Enhanced borrower protections implemented
- Monthly complaint snapshots spotlight mortgage sector regularly

## Lead Gen Implications

Mortgage complaint spikes signal consumers who are:
- Struggling with current servicers (refinance leads)
- Facing payment difficulty (debt counseling leads)
- Seeking better servicing terms (lender comparison leads)

Rate hike periods are historically the highest-volume periods for mortgage-related lead generation.
