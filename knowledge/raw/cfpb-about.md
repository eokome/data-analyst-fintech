**Source URL:** https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/  
**Scraped:** 2026-04-27

## Mission and Purpose

The CFPB is "a 21st century agency that implements and enforces Federal consumer financial law and ensures that markets for consumer financial products are transparent, fair, and competitive."

The organization's core objective is to make consumer financial markets function effectively for consumers, responsible financial providers, and the broader economy.

## Main Work Areas

The Bureau focuses on:
- Enforcing federal consumer financial regulations
- Protecting consumers through legal action against violations
- Publishing enforcement data and compensation information for harmed consumers

## Strategic Priorities

The CFPB maintains transparency through public documents:
- Strategic planning documents outlining organizational priorities
- Financial reports and updates showing how public resources support the mission
- Budget disclosures for accountability to American citizens

## Collaboration and Partnerships

The organization actively engages with:
- Subject matter experts and external stakeholders
- Third-party vendors for policy development
- Advisory committees
- Government contractors and procurement partners

## Employment and Operations

The CFPB maintains a career-focused workplace culture, actively recruiting talented individuals interested in consumer protection work.
